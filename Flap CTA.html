Abdominal Wall CTA (DIEP) 1-15-2018

We have been getting several referrals for CTA of the adominal wall for rectus adominus flaps for breast reconstruction to evaluate the number and location of deep inferior epigastric artery perforators (CTA DIEP). Main surgeon is Dr. Cody, whom I spoke with. We will add a new protocol to the manual after the next committee meeting on Feb 2. When reading these cases use Terarecon to create some MIP (thickness of at least 20mm) and/or Volume rendered images of the abdominal wall vessels. I will try to get the techs to create the majority of the MIPS in the future. The surgeon needs to know about the small perforating cephalad oriented branches from the inferior epigastric arteries on both sides and location relative to the umbilicus. Give some basic info in your report about these vessels, which could include number on each side, maximum caliber and lateral distance to the umbilicus. Dr. Cody and other surgeons will look at the images and make their own conclusions, so the reconstructed images are most important rather than the details of the report.
